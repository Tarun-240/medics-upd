About Nasal and Sinus Cancer

Nasal and sinus cancer are rare cancers that occur in the nasal cavity (the space behind your nose) and the paranasal sinuses (small, hollow spaces in the bones of your head). These cancers account for a small percentage of all cancers.

Symptoms

Symptoms of nasal and sinus cancer can be similar to those of common colds or sinus infections, but they persist and worsen. They may include:

  • Nasal congestion or blockage
  • Sinus pressure or pain
  • Nosebleeds
  • Loss of smell
  • Facial pain or swelling
  • Headaches
  • Watery eyes
  • Numbness or tingling in the face
  • A lump or sore inside the nose
  • Difficulty opening the mouth

Causes

The exact causes of nasal and sinus cancer are not fully understood, but several risk factors have been identified:

  • Tobacco smoke: Smoking significantly increases the risk.
  • Occupational exposure: Exposure to certain substances in the workplace, such as wood dust, leather dust, textile dust, nickel, chromium, formaldehyde, and solvents.
  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection: Some nasal and sinus cancers are associated with HPV.
  • Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection: EBV is associated with a specific type of nasal and sinus cancer.
  • Age: The risk increases with age.
  • Gender: Nasal and sinus cancers are more common in men.

Diagnosing

Diagnosing nasal and sinus cancer may involve:

  • Physical exam
  • Endoscopy (using a thin, flexible tube with a camera)
  • Imaging tests:
    • CT scan (computed tomography)
    • MRI (magnetic resonance imaging)
  • Biopsy (removing tissue for examination)

Prevention

While it's not always possible to prevent nasal and sinus cancer, you can reduce your risk by:

  • Avoiding tobacco smoke
  • Using protective equipment in workplaces with hazardous exposures
  • Getting the HPV vaccine

Treating

Treatment for nasal and sinus cancer depends on the stage and location of the cancer. Options include:

  • Surgery (to remove the tumor)
  • Radiation therapy (to destroy cancer cells)
  • Chemotherapy (to kill cancer cells)
  • Targeted therapy
  • Immunotherapy
