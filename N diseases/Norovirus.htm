About Norovirus

Norovirus is a highly contagious virus that causes gastroenteritis, an illness that involves inflammation of the stomach and intestines. It is often called the "stomach flu," although it is not related to the influenza virus. Norovirus can spread rapidly and cause outbreaks in crowded places like hospitals, nursing homes, schools, and cruise ships.

Symptoms

Symptoms of norovirus infection usually begin 12 to 48 hours after exposure and typically last for 1 to 3 days. They can include:

  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Stomach cramps
  • Fever (low-grade)
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches

Causes

Norovirus infection is caused by the norovirus. It spreads easily through:

  • Consuming contaminated food or water
  • Touching contaminated surfaces and then putting your unwashed hands in your mouth
  • Having direct contact with an infected person

Norovirus is highly contagious because infected people shed a large number of virus particles in their vomit and stool.

Diagnosing

Diagnosing norovirus infection is usually based on the symptoms. However, it can be confirmed by:

  • Stool test (to detect the virus)

Prevention

Preventing norovirus infection involves:

  • Washing your hands frequently and thoroughly with soap and water, especially after using the toilet, changing diapers, and before preparing or eating food
  • Cleaning and disinfecting contaminated surfaces
  • Washing laundry thoroughly
  • Handling food safely (washing fruits and vegetables, cooking food thoroughly)
  • Staying home when you are sick and for at least 2 days after symptoms stop

Treating

There is no specific medicine to treat norovirus infection. Treatment focuses on:

  • Drinking plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration
  • Getting plenty of rest
  • Eating bland foods

Treating

  • Hydration
  • Rest
  • Supportive Care
