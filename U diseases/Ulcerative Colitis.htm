About Ulcerative Colitis

Ulcerative colitis (UC) is a chronic inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) that causes inflammation and ulcers in the innermost lining of the large intestine (colon) and rectum. It is an autoimmune condition.

Symptoms

Symptoms of ulcerative colitis can vary in severity and may come and go. Common symptoms include:

  • Diarrhea, often with blood or pus
  • Abdominal pain and cramping
  • Rectal pain
  • Rectal bleeding
  • Urgency to defecate
  • Inability to defecate despite urgency
  • Weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Fever
  • Anemia

Causes

The exact cause of ulcerative colitis is unknown, but it is likely a combination of factors:

  • Autoimmune reaction: The immune system mistakenly attacks the lining of the colon and rectum.
  • Genetic predisposition: People with a family history of IBD are at higher risk.
  • Environmental factors: Factors such as certain bacteria or viruses may trigger the disease.

Diagnosing

Diagnosing ulcerative colitis typically involves a combination of tests and procedures:

  • Colonoscopy: To examine the colon and rectum and take biopsies.
  • Biopsy: Tissue samples from the colon are examined under a microscope.
  • Blood tests: To check for anemia and inflammation.
  • Stool tests: To rule out infections.
  • Imaging tests: X-rays or CT scans (if complications are suspected).

Prevention

There is no known way to prevent ulcerative colitis. However, certain things can help manage the condition and reduce flare-ups:

  • Following your treatment plan
  • Managing stress
  • Eating a healthy diet
  • Staying hydrated

Treating

Treatment for ulcerative colitis aims to control inflammation, relieve symptoms, and prevent complications. Treatment options include:

  • Medications:
    • Anti-inflammatory drugs (aminosalicylates)
    • Corticosteroids
    • Immunomodulators
    • Biologics
  • Surgery: To remove the colon and rectum (in severe cases).
  • Nutritional support
  • Stress management
