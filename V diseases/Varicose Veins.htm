About Varicose Veins

Varicose veins are enlarged, twisted veins. They often appear swollen and raised, and usually occur on the legs and feet. They are caused by faulty valves in the veins that prevent blood from flowing properly.

Symptoms

Varicose veins may not cause any pain. But some people may experience:

  • Aching, heavy, or throbbing legs
  • Swelling in the legs and feet
  • Worsened pain after sitting or standing for a long time
  • Itchy or irritated veins
  • Visible, twisted, swollen veins

Causes

Varicose veins are caused by weak or damaged valves in the veins. Arteries carry blood from your heart to the rest of your body. Veins return blood from your body to your heart. To keep blood flowing toward the heart, veins have valves that act as one-way flaps. If these valves are weak or damaged, blood can flow backward and pool in the veins, causing them to stretch or twist.

Several factors increase your risk of developing varicose veins:

  • Age: The risk increases with age.
  • Sex: Women are more likely to develop the condition.
  • Pregnancy: Increased blood volume during pregnancy can enlarge veins.
  • Family history: A family history of varicose veins increases your risk.
  • Obesity: Excess weight puts more pressure on veins.
  • Standing or sitting for long periods: This can increase pressure in veins.

Diagnosing

A doctor can usually diagnose varicose veins by a physical examination.

Additional tests may be needed:

  • Duplex ultrasound: To check blood flow and look for blood clots.

Prevention

There's no way to completely prevent varicose veins. However, some measures can help reduce your risk:

  • Exercise regularly: To improve blood circulation.
  • Maintain a healthy weight: To reduce pressure on veins.
  • Elevate your legs: To help blood flow back to the heart.
  • Avoid standing or sitting for long periods.
  • Wear compression stockings.

Treating

Treatment for varicose veins aims to relieve symptoms, improve appearance, and prevent complications.

Treatment options include:

  • Compression stockings: To improve blood flow.
  • Sclerotherapy: To close off small and medium-sized varicose veins.
  • Laser therapy: To close off small varicose veins.
  • Ambulatory phlebectomy: To remove smaller varicose veins.
  • Endovenous ablation: To close varicose veins with heat.
  • Surgery: To remove or tie off large varicose veins.
