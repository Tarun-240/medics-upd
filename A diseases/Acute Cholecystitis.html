Acute Cholecystitis

About Acute Cholecystitis

Acute cholecystitis is a sudden inflammation of the gallbladder, a small, pear-shaped organ located in the upper right abdomen. The gallbladder stores and releases bile, a digestive fluid produced by the liver.

The condition is often caused by gallstones that block the cystic duct, the tube through which bile flows in and out of the gallbladder. This blockage leads to a buildup of bile, causing inflammation and pain.

Symptoms of Acute Cholecystitis

Symptoms of acute cholecystitis can vary in intensity and may include:

  • Severe pain in the upper right abdomen that may radiate to the back or right shoulder
  • Tenderness to the touch in the upper right abdomen
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fever
  • Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes) in some cases

Causes of Acute Cholecystitis

The most common cause of acute cholecystitis is:

  • Gallstones: Hard deposits that form in the gallbladder.

Less common causes include:

  • Tumors blocking the bile duct
  • Bile duct strictures (narrowing)
  • Infections
  • Reduced blood flow to the gallbladder

Diagnosing Acute Cholecystitis

Diagnosing acute cholecystitis typically involves a combination of:

  • Physical examination: Checking for tenderness in the upper right abdomen.
  • Blood tests: To look for signs of infection or inflammation.
  • Imaging tests:
    • Ultrasound: To visualize gallstones and gallbladder inflammation.
    • HIDA scan (cholescintigraphy): To check gallbladder function and look for blockages.
    • CT scan: To rule out other complications or conditions.

Treating Acute Cholecystitis

Treatment for acute cholecystitis aims to relieve pain and inflammation, and prevent complications. Treatment options include:

  • Hospitalization: For monitoring and treatment.
  • Fasting: To reduce gallbladder stimulation.
  • Intravenous fluids: To prevent dehydration.
  • Pain medication: To control pain.
  • Antibiotics: To treat infection.
  • Cholecystectomy (gallbladder removal surgery): Usually necessary to resolve the condition. Can be done laparoscopically (minimally invasive) or through open surgery.
  • Percutaneous gallbladder drainage: A temporary procedure to drain the gallbladder if surgery needs to be delayed.

Prevention of Acute Cholecystitis

While it's not always possible to prevent acute cholecystitis, especially if it's caused by gallstones, some measures can help reduce the risk of developing gallstones and related gallbladder problems:

  • Maintain a healthy weight: Obesity is a risk factor for gallstones.
  • Eat a healthy diet: A diet low in fat and high in fiber can help prevent gallstones.
  • Exercise regularly: Physical activity can help prevent gallstone formation.
  • Avoid rapid weight loss: Very rapid weight loss can increase the risk of gallstones.
  • Manage cholesterol levels: High cholesterol can contribute to gallstone formation.
