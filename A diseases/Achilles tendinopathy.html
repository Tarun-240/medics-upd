Achilles Tendinopathy

About Achilles Tendinopathy

Achilles tendinopathy is a condition that causes pain, swelling, and stiffness of the Achilles tendon, the large tendon that runs down the back of your lower leg and connects your calf muscles to your heel bone.

It's a common condition, particularly in athletes and active individuals, but it can affect anyone. The pain can range from mild to severe and can significantly impact daily activities and sports performance.

Symptoms of Achilles Tendinopathy

The main symptom of Achilles tendinopathy is pain along the back of the heel and lower leg. Other symptoms can include:

  • Pain that worsens with activity
  • Stiffness, especially in the morning or after rest
  • Tenderness to the touch
  • Swelling or a bump on the tendon
  • A creaking or crackling sensation when moving the ankle
  • Weakness in the affected leg

Causes of Achilles Tendinopathy

Achilles tendinopathy is usually caused by overuse or repetitive strain on the tendon. Other contributing factors can include:

  • Sudden increase in activity level or intensity
  • Tight calf muscles
  • Inadequate stretching before exercise
  • Wearing inappropriate footwear
  • Running on hard or uneven surfaces
  • Age-related degeneration of the tendon
  • Certain medical conditions, such as arthritis

Diagnosing Achilles Tendinopathy

A healthcare professional can usually diagnose Achilles tendinopathy based on a physical examination and your description of symptoms. They will likely:

  • Ask about your medical history and activity levels
  • Physically examine your Achilles tendon, checking for pain, swelling, and tenderness
  • Assess your range of motion and strength

In some cases, imaging tests like ultrasound or MRI may be used to rule out other conditions or assess the extent of tendon damage.

Prevention of Achilles Tendinopathy

You can take steps to help prevent Achilles tendinopathy, such as:

  • Gradually increasing your activity level
  • Stretching your calf muscles regularly, especially before and after exercise
  • Wearing supportive and appropriate footwear for your activities
  • Avoiding running on hard or uneven surfaces
  • Listening to your body and stopping activities if you feel pain
  • Maintaining a healthy weight

Treating Achilles Tendinopathy

The goal of treatment is to reduce pain and inflammation and restore the function of the Achilles tendon.

  • Rest and activity modification
  • Ice application
  • Pain medication (NSAIDs)
  • Physical therapy (stretching, strengthening)
  • Orthotics (heel lifts)
  • Injections (corticosteroids, PRP - sometimes)
  • Surgery (rarely)
