Chilblains

About Chilblains

Chilblains are small, itchy, red or purple bumps that occur on the skin, usually in response to cold, damp conditions. They most often affect the fingers and toes, but can also appear on the nose, ears, and cheeks.

The exact cause of chilblains is not fully understood, but they are thought to be caused by abnormal blood vessel reaction to cold. When the skin is exposed to cold, blood vessels narrow (vasoconstriction) to conserve heat. If the skin is then warmed too quickly, the blood vessels may not be able to adjust properly, leading to blood leakage and inflammation.

Symptoms of Chilblains

Symptoms of chilblains can include:

  • Small, red or purple bumps on the skin
  • Itching
  • Burning sensation
  • Pain
  • Swelling
  • Numbness
  • In severe cases, blisters or sores may develop

Symptoms usually appear within hours of exposure to cold and may worsen with warmth.

Causes of Chilblains

Chilblains are primarily caused by repeated exposure to cold, damp conditions. Specific factors that contribute include:

  • Exposure to cold, damp weather
  • Poor circulation
  • Tight-fitting shoes or clothing

Individuals with certain conditions are more susceptible to chilblains:

  • Raynaud's phenomenon
  • Lupus
  • Anemia
  • Peripheral vascular disease
  • Diabetes

Diagnosing Chilblains

Chilblains are usually diagnosed based on their appearance and the patient's history of exposure to cold.

A doctor may perform the following:

  • Physical examination: To observe the characteristic skin lesions.
  • Medical history: To gather information about symptoms, cold exposure, and other medical conditions.

Treating Chilblains

Treatment for chilblains focuses on relieving symptoms, promoting healing, and preventing recurrence.

Treatment options include:

  • Self-care measures:
    • Keep the affected areas warm and dry: Avoid sudden temperature changes.
    • Elevate the affected areas: To reduce swelling.
    • Avoid scratching: To prevent infection.
    • Gently massage the affected areas: To improve circulation (but avoid rubbing vigorously).
  • Medications:
    • Corticosteroid creams: To reduce inflammation and itching.
    • Nifedipine: A calcium channel blocker that can improve blood flow (for severe cases or when self-care measures are ineffective).
    • Pentoxifylline: Can also help improve blood flow.
    • Antibiotics: If the chilblains become infected.

Prevention of Chilblains

Preventing chilblains is the best approach. Here are some preventive measures:

  • Avoid prolonged exposure to cold and dampness.
  • Dress warmly in cold weather: Wear gloves, thick socks, and layered clothing.
  • Keep your feet and hands dry.
  • Warm your skin gradually: Avoid sudden exposure to heat.
  • If you smoke, quit: Smoking impairs circulation.
  • Exercise regularly: To improve circulation.
  • Moisturize your skin: To prevent dryness and cracking.
