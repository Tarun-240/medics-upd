About Zika Virus

Zika virus is a mosquito-borne flavivirus. It is primarily transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes. Zika virus infection can cause mild symptoms in most people, but it poses a significant risk to pregnant women due to its association with microcephaly in infants.

Symptoms

Many people infected with Zika virus do not develop any symptoms. When symptoms do occur, they are usually mild and last for several days to a week. They may include:

  • Fever
  • Rash
  • Headache
  • Joint pain
  • Muscle pain
  • Conjunctivitis (red eyes)

Causes

Zika virus infection is primarily caused by the bite of an infected Aedes mosquito. The virus can also be transmitted:

  • From mother to fetus during pregnancy
  • Through sexual contact
  • Through blood transfusion (less common)

Diagnosing

Zika virus infection can be diagnosed through:

  • Blood or urine tests (to detect the virus or antibodies)

Prevention

Preventing Zika virus infection primarily relies on:

  • Mosquito control (reducing mosquito populations and breeding grounds)
  • Personal protective measures (using insect repellent, wearing long sleeves and pants)
  • Prevention of sexual transmission (using condoms)
  • Vaccination (when available) - Note: A vaccine is not yet widely available, but research is ongoing.

Treating

There is no specific antiviral treatment for Zika virus infection. Treatment focuses on relieving symptoms:

  • Rest
  • Fluids
  • Pain relief (for fever and aches)
