About Bowel Incontinence

Bowel incontinence is the loss of ability to control bowel movements. This can range from occasionally leaking a small amount of stool or gas to a complete loss of bowel control. It can have significant impact on a person's quality of life.

Symptoms

Symptoms of bowel incontinence may include:

  • Inability to delay bowel movements
  • Leakage of stool when passing gas
  • Accidental passing of stool
  • Urgency (a sudden, strong need to defecate)

Causes

Bowel incontinence can be caused by various factors, including:

  • Muscle damage (e.g., from childbirth or surgery)
  • Nerve damage (e.g., from diabetes, multiple sclerosis)
  • Severe constipation
  • Diarrhea
  • Hemorrhoids
  • Rectal prolapse

Diagnosing

Diagnosing bowel incontinence may involve:

  • Physical examination
  • Medical history review
  • Digital rectal exam
  • Anorectal manometry (to test muscle function)
  • Imaging tests (e.g., ultrasound, MRI)
  • Colonoscopy

Prevention

While not always preventable, some measures can help reduce the risk or manage the condition:

  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Eating a high-fiber diet
  • Drinking plenty of fluids
  • Exercising regularly
  • Performing Kegel exercises
  • Promptly treating diarrhea or constipation

Treating

Treatment for bowel incontinence depends on the cause and severity, and may include:

  • Dietary changes
  • Medications
  • Bowel training
  • Pelvic floor exercises
  • Surgery
  • Fecal incontinence devices
