About Bladder Cancer

Bladder cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the cells of the bladder. The bladder is a hollow, muscular organ in the lower abdomen that stores urine. Most bladder cancers are diagnosed early, when they are highly treatable.

Symptoms

Bladder cancer symptoms may include:

  • Blood in urine (hematuria), which may appear bright red or cola-colored
  • Painful urination
  • Frequent urination
  • Urinary urgency
  • Pain in the lower abdomen

Causes

Bladder cancer is most often caused by:

  • Smoking
  • Exposure to certain chemicals

Other risk factors include:

  • Age (risk increases with age)
  • Being male
  • Chronic bladder infections
  • Family history of bladder cancer

Diagnosing

Diagnosing bladder cancer may involve:

  • Cystoscopy (using a thin tube with a camera to examine the bladder)
  • Urine cytology (examining urine cells under a microscope)
  • Biopsy (taking a tissue sample for examination)
  • Imaging tests (CT scan, MRI)

Prevention

While not all bladder cancers are preventable, you can reduce your risk by:

  • Not smoking
  • Avoiding exposure to certain chemicals
  • Drinking plenty of fluids
  • Following safety instructions when working with chemicals

Treating

Bladder cancer treatment depends on the stage and grade of the cancer, and may include:

  • Surgery (to remove the cancer)
  • Chemotherapy (to kill cancer cells)
  • Radiation therapy (to destroy cancer cells)
  • Immunotherapy (to help the body's immune system fight cancer)
  • Targeted therapy (to attack specific cancer cells)
