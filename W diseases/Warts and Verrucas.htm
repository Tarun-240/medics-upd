About Warts and Verrucas

Warts and verrucas are skin growths caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). They are common and can appear on various parts of the body. Verrucas specifically refer to warts found on the feet.

Symptoms

Symptoms of warts and verrucas include:

  • Flesh-colored, raised bumps
  • Rough texture
  • Tiny black dots (clotted blood vessels) may be visible
  • Pain when walking (for verrucas)

Causes

Warts and verrucas are caused by different strains of the human papillomavirus (HPV). The virus infects the top layer of skin, causing it to grow quickly and form a wart.

  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection
  • Direct contact with HPV

Verrucas are caused by the same virus as warts, but they grow on the soles of your feet. The pressure from walking flattens them and pushes them back into the skin.

Diagnosing

Warts and verrucas are usually diagnosed through:

  • Visual examination by a healthcare provider
  • Dermoscopy (sometimes used for closer inspection)

Prevention

You can reduce the risk of spreading warts and verrucas by:

  • Avoiding touching other people's warts
  • Keeping your feet clean and dry
  • Wearing flip-flops in public showers and pools
  • Covering warts with a bandage

Treating

Most warts and verrucas are harmless and eventually go away on their own. However, treatment can speed up the process. Treatment options include:

  • Topical medications (e.g., salicylic acid)
  • Cryotherapy (freezing)
  • Electrocautery (burning)
  • Curettage (scraping)
  • Laser treatment
