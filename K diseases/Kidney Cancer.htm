About Kidney Cancer

Kidney cancer, also known as renal cancer, is a disease in which malignant (cancer) cells form in the tubules of the kidney. The kidneys are two bean-shaped organs, each about the size of a fist, located on either side of the spine in the lower back. Their main function is to filter waste and excess fluid from the blood, which is then excreted in the urine.

Symptoms

In its early stages, kidney cancer often doesn't cause any symptoms. However, as the tumor grows, symptoms may develop. These can include:

  • Blood in the urine (hematuria)
  • Persistent back or side pain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Fever that doesn't go away
  • A lump or mass in the side or abdomen

Causes

The exact causes of kidney cancer are not fully understood, but several risk factors have been identified:

  • Smoking
  • Obesity
  • High blood pressure
  • Family history of kidney cancer
  • Advanced kidney disease or dialysis
  • Exposure to certain chemicals (e.g., asbestos, cadmium)
  • Certain genetic conditions (e.g., von Hippel-Lindau disease)

Diagnosing

Kidney cancer may be diagnosed through a variety of methods:

  • Urine tests (to check for blood in urine)
  • Blood tests (to assess kidney function)
  • Imaging tests:
    • CT scan (computed tomography)
    • MRI (magnetic resonance imaging)
    • Ultrasound
  • Biopsy (to examine tissue sample)

Prevention

While it's not possible to guarantee prevention, you can take steps to reduce your risk:

  • Quit smoking
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Control high blood pressure
  • Eat a healthy diet
  • Exercise regularly
  • Avoid exposure to harmful chemicals

Treating

Kidney cancer treatment options depend on several factors, including the stage of cancer, your overall health, and personal preferences. Treatment options include:

  • Surgery:
    • Nephrectomy (partial or radical)
  • Active surveillance
  • Ablation therapies (radiofrequency ablation, cryoablation)
  • Radiation therapy
  • Targeted therapy
  • Immunotherapy
