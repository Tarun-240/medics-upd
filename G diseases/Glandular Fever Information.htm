About Glandular Fever

Glandular fever, also known as infectious mononucleosis or mono, is an illness typically caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV). It's characterized by a combination of symptoms, most commonly sore throat, fever, and fatigue.

Symptoms

Symptoms of glandular fever can vary but often include:

  • Severe sore throat
  • High fever
  • Swollen lymph nodes in the neck and armpits
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Loss of appetite
  • Swollen tonsils

Causes

Glandular fever is primarily caused by the Epstein-Barr virus (EBV). The virus is most often spread through saliva, which is why it's sometimes called "the kissing disease."

  • Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) infection
  • Spread through saliva

Diagnosing

Diagnosing glandular fever typically involves:

  • Physical examination
  • Blood tests (Monospot test, complete blood count)
  • Review of symptoms

Prevention

There is no specific vaccine to prevent glandular fever. However, you can reduce your risk of infection by:

  • Practicing good hygiene (e.g., washing hands frequently)
  • Avoiding close contact with people who have glandular fever (e.g., not sharing drinks or utensils)

Treating

Treatment for glandular fever focuses on relieving symptoms, as there is no specific cure. Treatment options include:

  • Rest
  • Pain relievers (e.g., acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
  • Sore throat remedies (e.g., gargling with salt water)
  • Plenty of fluids
