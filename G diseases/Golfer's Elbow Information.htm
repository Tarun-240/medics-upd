About Golfer's Elbow

Golfer's elbow, also known as medial epicondylitis, is a condition that causes pain on the inside of your elbow where the tendons of your forearm muscles attach to the bony bump. While it's common in golfers, anyone can get it.

Symptoms

The most common symptoms of golfer's elbow include:

  • Pain and tenderness on the inside of your elbow
  • Stiffness in the elbow
  • Weakness in your hands and wrists
  • Numbness or tingling in your ring and little fingers
  • Pain that worsens with certain movements, such as gripping, making a fist, or swinging a golf club

Causes

Golfer's elbow is caused by damage to the muscles and tendons that control your wrist and fingers. The pain is primarily located where these tendons attach to the bony bump on the inside of your elbow. The damage may result from:

  • Repeatedly bending your wrist
  • Forcefully gripping something
  • Twisting or rotating your forearm

Activities that can lead to golfer's elbow include:

  • Golfing
  • Racket sports
  • Throwing sports
  • Weightlifting
  • Occupations involving repetitive hand and wrist movements (e.g., carpentry, plumbing, typing)

Diagnosing

Golfer's elbow is usually diagnosed through a:

  • Physical examination
  • Medical history review
  • Imaging tests (X-rays, MRI, or EMG) may be used in some cases to rule out other conditions.

Prevention

There are several steps you can take to prevent golfer's elbow:

  • Use proper form and technique in sports and activities.
  • Strengthen your forearm muscles.
  • Stretch before and after activities.
  • Use lighter weights or equipment.
  • Take breaks from activities that involve repetitive hand and wrist movements.

Treating

Treatment for golfer's elbow aims to relieve pain and restore function. Options include:

  • Rest
  • Ice
  • Pain medication (e.g., NSAIDs)
  • Physical therapy
  • Braces or splints
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • Surgery (in severe cases)
