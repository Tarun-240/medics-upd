About Genital Warts

Genital warts are small, fleshy growths on the genitals, rectum, or groin. They are caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV), a sexually transmitted infection (STI).

Symptoms

Genital warts may appear as:

  • Small, flesh-colored or gray bumps
  • Cauliflower-shaped growths
  • Itching or discomfort in the genital area
  • Bleeding with intercourse

Causes

Genital warts are caused by the human papillomavirus (HPV). HPV is spread through skin-to-skin contact, usually during sexual activity.

  • Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection
  • Sexual contact with an infected person

Diagnosing

Genital warts are usually diagnosed through:

  • Visual examination by a healthcare provider
  • Biopsy (if needed)
  • HPV testing (sometimes)

Prevention

You can reduce your risk of getting genital warts by:

  • Getting the HPV vaccine
  • Using condoms during sex
  • Having regular check-ups

Treating

Treatment options for genital warts include:

  • Topical medications (e.g., imiquimod, podophyllin)
  • Cryotherapy (freezing)
  • Electrocautery (burning)
  • Laser treatment
  • Surgical removal
